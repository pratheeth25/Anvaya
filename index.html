About Anvaya

ANVAYA is the prestigious annual sports and cultural extravaganza organised by the Satpantha Student Union 2025–26 of Sri Madhwa Yuvaka Sangha (R), Bengaluru.

Bringing together the finest talents for a celebration of art, intellect, and athletic prowess, Anvaya features an exciting lineup spanning cultural performances, intellectual challenges, e-sports, and classic sporting competitions.

From dumb charades and codethons to chess and badminton — there's something for everyone. Join us for an unforgettable celebration of talent, teamwork, and tradition.

Event Rules

Carrom

11th April · 9:00 AM onwards

General Instructions

  • 1.This is a singles event.
  • 2.Knockout format — lose a match and you're eliminated.
  • 3.Best of 3 boards per match.

Carrom Rules

  • 1.Standard Carrom Federation rules apply.
  • 2.Player with the white pieces strikes first in the first board.
  • 3.The striker must be placed within the baseline lines (the two circles define the boundary).
  • 4.The striker must touch both base lines or cross the front line.
  • 5.A coin pocketed from a penalty shot does not count; the opponent gets 1 point.
  • 6.Pocketing the Queen must be covered (followed by pocketing your own coin in the same turn).
  • 7.If you pocket the Queen but fail to cover, the Queen is returned to the centre.
  • 8.Pocketing the striker is a foul — return one of your pocketed coins & opponent gets a free shot.
  • 9.The last coin must not be pocketed before the Queen is covered.
  • 10.If you pocket your last coin before the Queen, it is a due — you owe the opponent 3 points.
  • 11.Board points: Winner earns points equal to the coins left by the loser + 5 points for the Queen (if covered by winner).
  • 12.Maximum 29 points per board; game continues to next board if a player reaches 29 first.

Umpire's Decision is Final

Singing

3rd & 4th April · Round 1: 11:30 AM (3rd)  |  Final: 6:30 PM (4th)

Format — Two Rounds

  • Round 1 —Solo performance, up to 3.5 minutes. Top performers qualify for the final.
  • Round 2 —Final round, 5–6 minutes. Any song of the participant's choice.

  • 2.Tanpura / shruti box allowed if needed — participant must arrange it themselves.

  • 5.The judge's decision is final.

Event Rules

Chess

11th April · 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM

Format

Rapid Chess — 10 minutes per player + 1 second increment per move. Swiss system rounds followed by knockouts.

Rules

  • 1.FIDE laws of chess apply throughout.
  • 2.Touch-move rule is strictly enforced — if you touch a piece you must move it.
  • 3.Illegal moves: first illegal move results in 2 minutes added to opponent's clock; second illegal move is a loss.
  • 4.Mobile phones must be switched off or on silent and kept away from the board.
  • 5.No consulting any material, devices, or other persons during the game.
  • 6.Draw can be claimed by repetition, 50-move rule, or mutual agreement after 30 moves.
  • 7.Unsportsmanlike conduct leads to immediate disqualification.
  • 8.Arbiter's decision is final.

Event Rules

Table Tennis

11th April · 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM

General

  • 1.Singles event — knockout format.
  • 2.Best of 5 games; each game to 11 points.
  • 3.ITTF rules apply.

Service Rules

  • 1.The ball must rest on the open palm of the free hand, above table level.
  • 2.The ball must be tossed upward at least 16 cm and struck on its way down.
  • 3.The ball must be behind the end line and visible to the umpire at all times during service.

Scoring

  • 1.Service alternates every 2 points.
  • 2.At 10–10 (deuce), service alternates every point until a 2-point lead is achieved.
  • 3.A let (net touch) on service is replayed; no limit on lets.

Event Rules

Badminton

12th April · 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Venue & Equipment

  • •Venue: Sri Nadaprabhu Kempegowda Indoor Stadium
  • •Shuttlecock: Yonex Mavis 200i (provided by organisers)
  • •Non-marking court shoes are mandatory.

Format

Singles — 15-point knockout. Best of 3 games per match.

Rules

  • 1.BWF laws of badminton apply.
  • 2.Toss decides service and side; winner chooses either.
  • 3.Service must be diagonal and below the server's waist.
  • 4.Shuttle must land within the court boundaries.
  • 5.At 14-all, the player who first reached 14 may choose to play to 15 or 17.
  • 6.Players change ends after each game; in the third game, change at 8 points.
  • 7.A let may be called by the umpire for unforeseen interruptions.
  • 8.Unsportsmanlike behaviour leads to disqualification.
