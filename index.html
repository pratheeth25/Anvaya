About Anvaya

ANVAYA is the prestigious annual sports and cultural extravaganza organised by the Satpantha Student Union 2025–26 of Sri Madhwa Yuvaka Sangha (R), Bengaluru.

Bringing together the finest talents for a celebration of art, intellect, and athletic prowess, Anvaya features an exciting lineup spanning cultural performances, intellectual challenges, e-sports, and classic sporting competitions.

From dumb charades and codethons to chess and badminton — there's something for everyone. Join us for an unforgettable celebration of talent, teamwork, and tradition.

Event Rules

Carrom

11th April · Timings will be announced soon

General Instructions

  • •Equipment: Players are requested to bring their own striker if possible; strikers will be provided if unavailable.
  • •Singles & Doubles: These rules apply to both Singles and Doubles formats.
  • •Conduct: Players must maintain proper social conduct. Any violation or unsportsmanlike behavior will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • •Compliance: Any team or player violating the rules mentioned below will be disqualified immediately.

Gameplay Rules

  • 1.Queen Coverage: A player may only pocket and cover the Queen if they have already pocketed at least one piece of their assigned color.
  • 2.Premature Queen: If a player pockets the Queen before they are permitted to cover it, they lose their turn and the Queen is returned to the center.
  • 3.Failure to Cover: When a player pockets the Queen but fails to cover it on the subsequent shot, the Queen is returned as near as possible to the center circle by the opponent.
  • 4.Placement: Pieces returned to the center cannot be placed on top of other pieces; they must be kept within the main center circle.
  • 5.Striker & Piece Foul: If a piece and the striker are pocketed simultaneously, the player may either (a) return two of their pocketed pieces to the board and shoot again, or (b) return one pocketed piece to the board and lose the turn.
  • 6.Striker Foul: If only the striker is pocketed, the player loses one pocketed piece and their turn.
  • 7.Player Positioning: Players are not allowed to move their chairs or stand up while the game is in progress.
  • 8.Board Contact: Only the player's hand/wrist is allowed to touch the carrom board. No other part of the body may rest on or touch the board.
  • 9.Fixed Sides: Players are not allowed to change sides once the game has commenced.
  • 10.Opponent's Piece: Sinking an opponent's piece results in a loss of turn. If you sink the opponent's last piece, you lose the board.
  • 11.Sequence Foul: If you sink your last piece before the Queen has been covered, you lose the board.
  • 12.Queen & Opponent Piece: If a player pockets the Queen and an opponent's piece in the same shot, the Queen is placed back on the board and the player loses their turn.

Umpire's Decision is Final

Note: Chess and Carrom will be conducted simultaneously on 11th April.

Event Rules

Table Tennis

11th April · 2:00 PM onwards

All rules are subject to change at the discretion of the SMYS administrators. TT Racquets are supposed to be brought by the players.

Single Player

I. General Rules

  • 1.The choice of playing position at the table and order of service is determined by the toss of a coin. If the winner of the toss prefers to have the first choice of playing positions, the opponent then has the choice of whether to serve first or receive first, and vice versa.
  • 2.Games are played to 11 points. Players serve two serves each, alternating. A player does not have to win specifically off their own serve in order to win a point. If a game ties at 10-10, a player must win by 2 points. In this situation, players serve one serve each, alternating.
  • 3.At the start of a new game, the player who served first in the previous game becomes the receiver and the receiver becomes the server and so on, alternating after each game.
  • 4.The player's exchange ends after each game, and if play consists of more than one game, in the deciding game of the match the player's exchange ends when one player reaches a score of 6 points.
  • 5.A match consists of the best two (2) of three (3) games.

II. Service

  • 1.A good service is delivered by projecting the ball up straight, from a flat palm from the free hand, which must start from above the playing surface. Toss and service contact must be behind the table surface (not over). Cannot hide the ball from the ball toss to contact, with any part of your body.
  • 2.As it starts to descend, the ball is struck so that it touches the server's court first and then, passing directly over or around the net touches the receiver's court. At the instant of contact of the racquet on the ball in service, both handle and ball must be behind the end line of the server's court.
  • 3.A good return of a served ball must be struck by the receiver on the first bounce so that it passes directly over or around the net and touches directly on top of the opponent's court.

III. Points

  • 1.A point is awarded to the opponent in the following circumstances:
  • 2.Failure to make a good service, unless a let is declared.
  • 3.Failure to make a good return of a good service or a good return made by the opponent, unless a let is declared.
  • 4.If the player, the racquet, or anything that the player wears or carries touches the net or its supports while the ball is in play.
  • 5.If the player, the racquet, or any wearing apparel moves the playing surface while the ball is in play or touches the net or its supports.
  • 6.If the player's free hand touches the playing surface while the ball is in play.
  • 7.If, after being struck by the opponent, the ball comes in contact with the player or anything the player wears or carries before it has passed over the end lines or sidelines, not yet having touched the playing surface on the player's side of the table.
  • 8.If at any time the player volleys the ball — that is before the ball hits the tabletop — except as provided in Rule A under "Let" (below).

IV. Let

  • 1.A let ball, which is then replayed, is called in the following cases:
  • 2.If the served ball, in passing over the net, touches it or its supports, if the service would otherwise have been good or volleyed by the receiver.
  • 3.If a service is delivered when the receiver is not ready.
  • 4.If either player is prevented by an accident not under his control from serving a good service or making a good return.
  • 5.If either player gives up a point, as provided in Rules C-G under "Points", owing to an accident not within his control.

V. Scoring

  • 1.A point is scored by the side that makes the last successful return prior to the end of a rally. An unsuccessful return occurs whenever the ball is missed, is hit off the table, sent into the net, or hit onto the player's own half of the court on the return.
  • 2.Failure to make a good serve also scores a point for the opponent unless it is a let.

VI. Ball in Play

  • 1.The ball is in play from the moment it is projected from the hand in service until one of the following has occurred:
  • 2.It has touched one court twice consecutively.
  • 3.It has, except in service, touched each court alternately without having been struck by the racquet immediately.
  • 4.It has been struck by either player more than once consecutively.
  • 5.It has touched either the player or anything that the player wears or carries.
  • 6.It has touched any object other than the net and supports.

Doubles Play

I. Good Service

  • 1.The service is delivered as described in Singles, except that it must touch first the right half of the server's court or the centreline on the server's side of the net and then, passing directly over or around the net, touch the right half of the receiver's court or the centreline on the receiver's side of the table.

II. Choice of Order of Play

  • 1.The team winning a coin toss has the option of choosing:
  • 2.The choice of ends.
  • 3.The right to receive or serve first.
  • 4.The right to require the losing team to make the first choice.
  • 5.After choices A & B have been made, the other team makes the remaining choice.

III. Order of Service

  • 1.The first two services must be delivered by the serving partner (Player 1) of the pair who has the right to do so. The service must be received by the receiving partner (Player 3) of the opposing pair.
  • 2.The second two services must be delivered by the receiver of the first two services (Player 3) and received by the partner of the server (Player 2) of the first two services.
  • 3.The third two services must be delivered by the partner of the first two services (Player 2) and received by the partner of the server (Player 4) of the first two services.
  • 4.The fourth two services must be delivered by the partner of the receiver (Player 4) of the first two services and received by the server (Player 1) of the first two services.
  • 5.The next two services start over as in Rule A of "Order of Service".

Event Rules

Badminton

12th April · 10:00 AM onwards

Venue & Equipment

  • •Venue: M.N. Krishna Rao Park Badminton Court, Basavanagudi, Bengaluru
  • •All players must carry a pair of non-marking shoes to be worn after entering the court, as required by the facility management.
  • •Shuttlecock: Yonex Mavis 200i will be provided by the organisers.

Format

  • •Entire tournament will be conducted in a knockout format.
  • •Each match consists of one set played to 15 points.
  • •For the finals, number of rounds/sets may be decided on the spot depending on time.

Rules

  • 1.Players must bring their own rackets. Rackets will not be provided by the organizers.
  • 2.Each game begins with a coin toss. Winner chooses to serve/receive or side of court.
  • 3.If both players reach 14-all, the first to gain a 2-point lead wins.
  • 4.A serve must travel diagonally to the opponent's service court.
  • 5.During the serve, the shuttlecock must be struck below the waist (applicable only on appeal).
  • 6.Players must not touch the net with their racket or body at any time.
  • 7.If the serving side wins a rally, they score and the same server continues from the alternate court.
  • 8.If the receiving side wins a rally, they score and become the new serving side.
  • 9.Players do not change service courts until they win a point while serving.
  • 10.All general badminton rules apply.
  • 11.Abusive language or discouraging remarks by players or supporters is strictly prohibited.
  • 12.The umpire's decision is final. Arguments will not be entertained.
  • 13.Players must report 30 minutes before the match. Late arrivals will not be allowed to enter.
